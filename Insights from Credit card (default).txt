Key Insights
1. Credit Utilization and Default Risk

High credit utilization is strongly associated with increased default probability.
This was confirmed using Welch’s t-test (p-value < 0.05), indicating a statistically significant difference between groups.

2. Payment Delay Trends

A significant relationship was found between repayment delay trends and default behavior (Pearson correlation, p-value < 0.05).
Interestingly, the negative correlation suggests a non-linear or counterintuitive relationship that may require deeper segmentation analysis.

3. Bill Amount Volatility

Defaulting customers exhibit lower bill volatility compared to non-defaulters.
This difference is statistically significant based on Welch’s t-test, suggesting more stable but potentially constrained spending behavior among defaulters.
